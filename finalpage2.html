    Wrong guesses

    This first graph has a Y axis labeled “# of games played with # of guesses” that tells the player how many games they played with the number of (wrong) guesses listed on the X axis. Every time that the player plays a round of hangman, new additions to the bar graph are added to reflect the number of their wrong guesses.


    Win lose ratios

    The second graph, “Win lose ratios!”, has a Y axis named “# of games” and an X axis with labels saying “Win” and “Lose”. Every time that the player finishes a round of hangman, the win or lose bar will increase by one.

    What does this teach us?

    Shown on this website are 2 versions of the “Wrong guesses” and “Win lose ratios!” graphs. We found that these two sets of graphs show improvement after playing Hangman multiple times.


    Win lose ratios

    Set one contains graph 1: the result from game 1 and graph 2: the result from game 2. In the first set, the player won 60% of the time, but in the second set, the player won 100% of the time, showing a clear improvement.

    Wrong guesses

    As the player familiarizes themselves with playable words, the number of wrong guesses gradually decreases. The player had a decrease of 57.1429% from 7 guesses to 3 guesses, again showing a improvement as the player plays
